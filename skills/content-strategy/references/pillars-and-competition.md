# Content Pillars & Competitive Positioning — Hit Network 2026

## Hit Network's 5 Content Pillars

### Pillar Structure: Hub-and-Spoke Model

Each pillar = one definitive long-form guide (the hub) + 8-15 supporting cluster pieces (the spokes). Every cluster piece links back to the pillar. Pillar links to every cluster. This builds topical authority.

**Recommended cluster size:** 8–15 pieces. Under 8 = insufficient authority signal. Over 15 = dilution risk unless production can keep everything updated.

## Pillar Performance Measurement

Monthly per-pillar dashboard:

| Metric | What it tells you |
|--------|------------------|
| Organic sessions to pillar page | Is the pillar ranking? |
| Cluster aggregate traffic | Is the hub-and-spoke working? |
| Avg time on page (pillar) | Are readers consuming the depth? |
| Internal links clicked (hub → cluster) | Is the structure connecting? |
| YouTube watch time per pillar tag | Which pillars hold viewer attention? |
| Social shares per pillar | Which topics have virality? |
| Email click-throughs per pillar | Which drive newsletter action? |

**80/20 rule:** 2 of the 5 pillars will drive 80% of traffic. Identify them early and double down, but never abandon the others — topical authority requires consistent coverage across all pillars.

**Quarterly review trigger:** 2 consecutive quarters of declining organic traffic for a pillar = either refresh the content or reconsider the pillar's market relevance.

## Competitive Positioning

### Where Each Competitor Plays

| Competitor | Strength | Blind Spot |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| CoinDesk | Institutional credibility, regulation, macro | Accessibility, personality, retail/DeFi education |
| CoinTelegraph | Global coverage, beginner guides, visual identity | Depth for serious retail investors, actionable content |
| Decrypt | User-friendly, Web3/NFT/DeFi, clean | Entertainment, personality, community-first voice |
| The Block | Premium research, institutional | Accessibility, retail content, community engagement |
| Milk Road | Personality, newsletter, mass market (330K+ subs) | Deep analysis, DeFi, on-chain interpretation |

### Where Discover Crypto / Blockchain Basement Wins

**Gap 1: Personality + depth.** CoinDesk has depth, no personality. Milk Road has personality, no depth. The gap is "smart friend who knows DeFi inside out and tells it to you straight."

**Gap 2: YouTube-native crypto education tied to real community.** All major competitors are text-first. Nobody owns the YouTube-to-community pipeline at scale.

**Gap 3: Intermediate retail investors (2-4 years in crypto).** Most competitors serve beginners OR institutions. The intermediate retail investor — holds alts, participates in DeFi, wants to level up — is underserved everywhere.

**Gap 4: On-chain data translated for normal people.** Glassnode has the data. Nobody consistently translates it for retail at the right depth. "What the on-chain data is saying this week" is a series waiting to be built.

**Gap 5: Actionable macro + crypto intersection.** CoinDesk covers macro. But nobody consistently connects Fed policy, rates, and dollar data to specific crypto positioning in plain English, in a way that tells retail what to do with the information.

### Differentiation Rules

✅ Compete on: entertainment + education hybrid, community-informed content, speed of actionability, personality-led analysis, deep dives that stay accessible
❌ Don't compete on: institutional credibility (CoinDesk's moat), beginner simplification (Decrypt's moat)

### Competitive Content Gap Process (Run Monthly)

1. Ahrefs Content Gap tool → input CoinDesk, CoinTelegraph, Decrypt → find keywords they rank for (pos 1-20) that we don't → filter KD < 40, volume > 200/mo
2. "Also Ranks For" mining → take a competitor's top article → find their full keyword cluster → identify uncovered angles
3. YouTube comment mining → top competitor videos → sort by Top Comments → unanswered questions = content briefs
4. Reddit/Discord audit → r/CryptoCurrency, r/defi, r/ethfinance → repeated questions = content gaps
