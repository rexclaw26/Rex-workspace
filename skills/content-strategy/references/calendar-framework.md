# Content Calendar Framework — Hit Network 2026

## The 3-Layer Architecture

**Layer 1 — 6-Month Macro Calendar**
Strategic skeleton: major campaigns, product launches, regulatory events (FOMC, ETF decisions, protocol upgrades), crypto market cycle moments. Build quarterly, review monthly.

**Layer 2 — 4-Week Rolling Editorial Calendar**
Specific topics, formats, and publish dates. Lock 2-3 weeks in advance. Leave 20% of slots open for reactive/breaking news.

**Layer 3 — Daily/Weekly Execution Queue**
Draft → edit → approve → schedule. Tracked in content-pipeline.

---

## Monthly Cadence Template

| Week | Focus | Content Types |
|------|-------|--------------|
| Week 1 | Anchor Content | Long-form YouTube deep dive + pillar article |
| Week 2 | Cluster Content | Supporting articles, explainers, social amplification |
| Week 3 | Engagement + Community | Opinion pieces, Discord-driven topics, "state of the market" roundup |
| Week 4 | Evergreen Refresh | Update top-performing old content, create clips from month's videos |

---

## Topic Prioritization Process

1. Check prior month's top 10 by pageviews, watch time, and social shares
2. Identify which pillars underperformed → over-correct next month
3. Map upcoming market events onto calendar first (FOMC, ETF decisions, protocol launches, earnings)
4. Fill remaining slots: evergreen + trending topics
5. Reserve 15-20% capacity for reactive breaking news

**3-Factor Scoring Model:**

| Factor | Weight | Measure |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Search demand | 35% | Keyword search volume (Google Trends, Ahrefs) |
| Audience relevance | 35% | Matches persona + current market interest |
| Strategic timing | 30% | Tied to upcoming event or trend window |

Score 1-10 per factor. Topics scoring 7+ get scheduled. Under 5 get deprioritized (unless breaking news).

## Crypto-Specific Timing

**Best publication windows for crypto content:**
- Tuesday–Thursday: Highest engagement for long-form content
- Monday 7-9 AM PST: Weekly market analysis (audience is checking "what happened over the weekend")
- Wednesday: FOMC preview/recap when Fed week
- Sunday evening: "Week ahead" market preview

**Recurring calendar anchors to build around every month:**
- FOMC meeting (every 6 weeks — major engagement spike)
- CPI/PPI data releases (2nd or 3rd week of each month)
- Options expiry (last Friday of each month — BTC/ETH)
- Monthly on-chain report (first week — Glassnode, Artemis data drop)
- Quarterly: Earnings season (COIN, MSTR, NVDA — institutional crypto proxy stocks)

**Event-triggered content (always reactive, can be planned in advance):**
- ETF approval/denial announcements
- Protocol major upgrades (Ethereum hard forks, Solana upgrades)
- Fed Chair speeches and Congressional testimony
- Major regulatory bills advancing in Congress
- Exchange hacks or protocol exploits (critical speed)

## Audience Demand Signals — How to Read Them

**Discord (highest signal — read weekly):**
- Questions appearing 3+ times in a week = content brief
- High-reaction messages = emotional resonance signals
- Long threads (20+ replies) = strong topic demand
- First-hour questions after major news = article lead angles

**YouTube comments:**
- Sort competitor videos by "Top Comments" — most-liked = unanswered questions
- Build content around patterns across multiple videos

**Google Trends:**
- Run weekly: check all 6 pillars for rising breakout topics (100 = peak popularity)
- Compare to 12-month baseline — rising trends = timing opportunity
- "Related queries" > "Rising" = the specific long-tail angles to target

**X/Twitter engagement:**
- Which posts drove replies (not just likes)? Replies = strong demand
- Quote-tweets with opinions = "opinion-worthy" topics worth more coverage

**Search Console:**
- Weekly: queries driving impressions but low CTR = title/meta needs fixing
- Queries driving clicks but low position = content needs deeper treatment
- New queries appearing = emerging audience interest to build into calendar
