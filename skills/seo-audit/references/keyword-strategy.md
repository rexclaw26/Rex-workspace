## E-E-A-T & YMYL Requirements (Required Reading Before Keyword Work)

Crypto is YMYL. Every keyword we target carries E-E-A-T obligations. Before building a content calendar around any keyword, confirm the content can meet these standards:

### Minimum Sources Per Article Type
- News articles: 2–3 primary sources (official announcements, on-chain data, direct quotes)
- Analysis/opinion: 3–5 cited sources with links
- Evergreen guides ("How to Buy Bitcoin"): 5+ authoritative sources
- Price/market analysis: source tag on EVERY data point (CoinGecko, CoinMarketCap, Glassnode, etc.)
- Regulatory content: cite the actual legislation, not summaries of summaries
- All sources must be dated — undated sources = low trust for crypto

### Author Bio Requirements (Every Article)
1. Full real name (no "Staff Writer")
2. Professional title + relevant credentials (CFA candidate, former Coinbase dev, CPA, etc.)
3. Years of experience in crypto/finance specifically
4. Links to LinkedIn (required), Twitter/X, personal site
5. Conflict of interest disclosure ("The author holds BTC and ETH")
6. Editorial review note if reviewed by separate expert
7. Dedicated author page that Google can index — not just inline text

### Financial Disclaimer (Required on Every Article)
Must appear near the top of the article AND in the footer:
> "The information provided is for educational purposes only and should not be considered financial, investment, or legal advice. Cryptocurrency investments carry significant risk, including potential loss of all invested capital. Past performance is not indicative of future results. Always conduct your own research and consult a qualified financial advisor. Discover Crypto may hold positions in assets discussed."

### E-E-A-T Failure Triggers (Never Do These)
- Anonymous authorship on financial content
- Price predictions presented as facts without disclaimers
- No external citations or source links in articles
- Mixing speculation with factual reporting without clear labels
- Outdated market data presented as current
- No corrections policy on the site
- Missing About/Team page with real names and credentials

## Keyword Research Process

### Step 1 — Find Low-Competition Opportunities
1. Start broad: "DeFi protocols 2026"
2. In Ahrefs/Semrush: filter KD (Keyword Difficulty) under 30
3. Look for informational intent, 500–10,000 monthly searches
4. Check who ranks now — if it's exchanges/projects not media, we can compete
5. Look for question keywords (what, how, why, when) — easier to rank + AEO-friendly

### Step 2 — Intent Classification
| Intent | Example | Our play |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Informational | "what is restaking" | Yes — educational |
| News | "bitcoin price today" | Yes — breaking news/analysis |
| Commercial investigation | "best crypto exchanges 2026" | Case-by-case — editorial only |
| Transactional | "buy bitcoin cheapest" | No — not our business model |
| Navigational | "Discover Crypto YouTube" | Brand content |

### Step 3 — Competitor Gap Analysis
Run monthly: Ahrefs → Content Gap tool against these competitors:
- CoinDesk (coindesk.com)
- CoinTelegraph (cointelegraph.com)
- Decrypt (decrypt.co)
- The Block (theblock.co)
- BeInCrypto (beincrypto.com)

Find keywords they rank for (positions 1-20) that we don't rank for at all. Filter by KD < 40 and volume > 200/mo. These are your highest-priority content gaps.

### Keyword Difficulty Targets
| Our domain authority stage | Target KD range |
|---------------------------|----------------|
| Early / building (DA < 30) | KD 0–20 |
| Growing (DA 30–50) | KD 0–35 |
| Established (DA 50+) | KD 0–50 |

## AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) — Getting Cited by AI

In 2026, crypto discovery increasingly happens through ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. YMYL content that demonstrates high E-E-A-T gets cited most.

### Content Patterns AI Engines Prefer
1. **Question-format H2/H3 headings** — "What is liquid staking?" → answer directly below
2. **Direct answer in first 2-3 sentences** under each heading — don't bury the answer
3. **Definitions** — define every crypto term explicitly (AI engines pull definitions)
4. **FAQ sections** at article end — schema-marked with FAQPage JSON-LD
5. **Inline source citations** — AI engines cite content that cites sources
6. **Structured data** — well-marked content is easier for AI to extract
7. **Numbered lists and comparison tables** — AI engines parse these well

### FAQ Schema Implementation
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [{
    "@type": "Question",
    "name": "What is liquid staking?",
    "acceptedAnswer": {
      "@type": "Answer",
      "text": "Liquid staking allows users to stake cryptocurrency while receiving a liquid token representing their staked position..."
    }
  }]
}
```

### YMYL Note for AEO
AI engines apply extra scrutiny to financial content citations. Content that:
- Has named authors with credentials
- Cites primary sources (SEC.gov, coindesk.com, on-chain data)
- Includes financial disclaimers
- Has been published for 6+ months with no corrections flagged

...gets cited more reliably than anonymous or uncited content.

## Link Building for Crypto Media

### What Works (Legitimate)
- **Proprietary data studies** — TVL movements, fee analysis, on-chain metrics, chain adoption stats. Other media cites these.
- **Crypto glossaries** — comprehensive definitions of DeFi terms (restaking, MEV, account abstraction, zkEVM). These attract organic links and AI citations.
- **Comparison pages** — wallets, bridges, exchanges, staking platforms (editorial only, no affiliate bias)
- **Expert quotes** given to CoinDesk, The Block, Decrypt, Reuters, Bloomberg Crypto journalists
- **Named contributor podcast appearances** — builds both links and author E-E-A-T signals
- **Correction and transparency reporting** — being the most accurate source earns citations

### What to Avoid
| Tactic | Risk |
|--------|------|
| PBNs (Private Blog Networks) | Site-wide deindexing — Google detects footprints |
| Paid crypto link schemes | Sponsored links require rel=sponsored; paid dofollow = manual penalty risk |
| Low-quality crypto directories | Degrades link profile; disproportionately large spam tail in crypto niche |
| Heavy exact-match anchor text | Triggers algorithmic scrutiny on YMYL sites specifically |
| Bulk AI-generated guest posts | Google specifically targeting this at scale |

### Realistic Timelines
- **Months 1–3:** Technical foundation + topical clusters. Long-tail keywords hit top 20.
- **Months 3–6:** Editorial links + digital PR. Early visibility on lower-competition informational queries.
- **Months 6–12:** Compounding authority. Mid-competition keywords start moving.
- **Months 12–24:** Competitive rankings for high-value terms (Bitcoin, DeFi, crypto legislation).

**Key insight:** YMYL sites need more backlinks than non-YMYL sites for equivalent rankings.
