SEC Defines When Crypto Assets Become Investment Contracts

The SEC published a new classification framework for when crypto assets legally become securities. Five core categories based on issuer-driven profit expectations. They also released this separate from the March 11 SEC-CFTC Joint Harmonization Initiative MOU.

The MOU establishes coordinated oversight, examination, and enforcement. The goals are clear: clarify product definitions and reduce friction for dually registered exchanges. Staking, mining, and airdrops got classified outside securities law. That's huge because it means the base layer infrastructure stays unregulated.

This is regulatory clarity actually happening. Not promises. Actual framework.
