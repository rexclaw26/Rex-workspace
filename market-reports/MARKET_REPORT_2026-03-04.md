The crypto complex is well-positioned for this scenario because BTC is a geopolitical and inflation hedge regardless of which way real rates move.

 Kraken Gets Fed Master Account + Direct Payment System Access 
 Kraken has become the first crypto exchange to obtain a Fed master account, giving it direct access to Fedwire, FedNow, and the Federal Reserve's payment infrastructure. This isn't access through a banking intermediary; this is direct participation in the Fed's core payment rails. Fed Governor Waller championed the move, though Governor Barr dissented. The FDIC also eliminated the prior notification requirement that had been effectively gatekeeping crypto from banking access. This represents a structural shift in how regulators view crypto exchange banking: they're no longer treating it as a special case requiring supervision through legacy banking channels, but rather as infrastructure that can be trusted to participate directly in Fed payment systems. 
 The immediate practical impact is that Kraken gets "skinny accounts" with a $500M cap on balances held at the Fed, but Fedwire and FedNow access means Kraken can offer instant settlement to customers and participate in the Fed's 24/7/365 payment system. The principle here is what matters: the regulatory moat around Fed access is dissolving. Citi and Morgan Stanley are already expanding their crypto custody operations in parallel. This inflection point suggests that within 12-18 months, we'll see a material shift in how institutional capital flows through crypto exchanges. The ability to settle instantly through Fed rails removes counterparty risk and custodial delay, which was the last remaining technical advantage of legacy banking for institutional participants. That advantage just evaporated.
