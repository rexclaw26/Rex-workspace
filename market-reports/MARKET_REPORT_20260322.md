SEC and CFTC Draw the Line: 16 Crypto Assets Are Commodities, Not Securities

 
The crypto industry just got what it's been asking for since 2017. On March 17, the SEC and CFTC published a joint 68-page interpretive release that explicitly names 16 crypto assets as digital commodities. The list: Bitcoin, Ether, Solana, XRP, Dogecoin, Cardano, Avalanche, Chainlink, Polkadot, Hedera, Litecoin, Bitcoin Cash, Shiba Inu, Stellar, Tezos, and Aptos. Regulated by the CFTC, not the SEC.

 
But here's where it gets interesting. Protocol mining on PoW networks is now classified as administrative activity, not a securities transaction. Staking on PoS networks gets the same treatment across four models. That's a massive unlock for ETF issuers waiting for staking clarity before launching yield-bearing products.

 
The caveat? DeFi protocols, NFTs, and tokens not on the list remain in gray area. But it's the clearest line federal regulators have drawn, removing the enforcement sword that hung over exchanges and institutional allocators for years.

 

 
What this means:

 
Staking is confirmed as a non-securities activity. The ETF pipeline is unblocked for all 16 named assets. Exchanges can list without enforcement risk. This is the broadest expansion of regulated crypto access since the Bitcoin ETF approval in January 2024.

 

 

 
The Bigger Picture:

 
This ruling creates a two-tier regulatory framework: named commodities with clear rules, and everything else in limbo. Expect a flood of ETF filings for Solana, XRP, Chainlink, and other named assets. The race to become the first-mover ETF issuer for each commodity-classified token has officially begun.

CLARITY Act Stablecoin Yield: The Deal's Done, But Politics Just Got Messy

 
Senators Tillis and Alsobrooks confirmed an agreement in principle on stablecoin yield Friday, resolving the dispute that stalled the CLARITY Act since January. Passive rewards for holding a stablecoin: prohibited. Activity-based rewards tied to payments and usage: permitted.

 
The CLARITY Act is the most comprehensive crypto market structure legislation ever drafted. It defines commodity vs. security, establishes registration frameworks, and creates the regulatory scaffolding institutions need.

 
But Senate Republicans now want to attach community bank deregulation to the bill. That's a potential poison pill. The senators plan to circulate text to stakeholders this week. No text had been shared as of Friday.

 

 
What this means:

 
The yield compromise eliminates the last technical barrier. But the community bank rider introduces political risk that could miss the 2026 window entirely.

 

 

 
Why should you care?

 
Without market structure legislation, every crypto business operates under uncertainty. The CLARITY Act doesn't just clarify rules. It creates them. Passage would unlock institutional capital flows that dwarf the ETF wave.
